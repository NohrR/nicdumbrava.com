Material: Mild steel

Technical Scope:

● Face and square stock close to final dimensions
● Drill and tap required holes
● Machine features, slots, and profiles
● Complete optional broaching and surface grinding to final dimensions (bonus work)

● Sequenced operations to maximize efficiency while maintaining accuracy (including optional broaching + grinding)

Tools & Equipment

● Vertical mill, surface grinder, arbour press + broach kit

● Go/No-Go gauge used throughout production

Quality & Verification

● Verified dimensions after major operations and during setup changes
● Used a Go/No-Go gauge for compliance checks during machining and at project completion

Skate Sharpener

Systems: 3-phase power simulation, forward/reverse motor control, safety relay + E-stop + reset, 24 VDC controls

Theme: 3 phase motor control with timed interlocking forward and reverse control

Designed and built a control system simulating an automatic skate sharpener using forward/reverse indexing and mode-based timing. Implemented safety relay control to remove motion power immediately on stop conditions while keeping sensing active.

Technical Scope:

● Wire 3-phase motor power through breakers/contactors to simulate industrial motor control
● Implement forward/reverse control with electrical/mechanical interlocking
● Add mode selection (child/adult) affecting indexing time
● Use proximity sensor as a start, permissive and safety stop condition
● Integrate safety relay + E-stop with manual reset behaviour
● Separate and document power vs control schematics

Work Performed

● Wired 3-phase power circuits to contactors (kept de-energized during testing for safety)
● Built 24 VDC control logic for indexing and grinding motor enable
● Implemented mode-based indexing: 7 s (adult) / 3 s (child)
● Required part presence (prox) to start; immediate shutdown on part removal or stop
● Integrated safety relay so E-stop drops all motion power until manually reset

Safety Considerations

● E-stop drops motion power while allowing sensors to remain active (industry-aligned approach)
● Reset required after E-stop release before restarting the motion
